About

Hi! My name is Alisa Thomas and I am an Undergraduate Electrical Engineering student at The University of Texas at Dallas (UTD). I am passionate about using my hardware knowledge to develop exciting consumer technology to solve world problems. My commitment to continue bridging the gender gap of women leaders in the engineering profession is also a subject very close to my heart.

Toyota Co-Op

In the Spring of 2019, I Co-Oped with Toyota Motor North America at their Headquarters in Plano, Texas. I worked on the Connnected Technologies team as a Telematics Engineer. During my time there, I worked on testing and analyzing the then unreleased 2019 Telematics Unit, providing support for the older 2016+ unit, and performing benchmarking activities on other OEM Telematics Systems.

AT&T Internship

In the Summer of 2019, I interned with AT&T as a Network Engineer in Atlanta, Georgia. During my 10 weeks with AT&T, I worked on an AT&T Field Operations team to perform data analysis on technician time reports to optimize their working and training hours and participated in an Intern Innovation Challenge to incorporate 5G into a AR smart navigation application for grocery shopping.

Boeing Internship

In the Summer of 2020, I interned at Boeing as a Cabin and Network Systems Engineering Intern. In a virtual internship setting, I used Python to characterize the RF performace of the Wireless WAN Unit on the 777X's Onboard Network System.

SWE UTD

From Fall 2018 to Spring 2020, I served as the President of the UT Dallas section of the Society of Women Engineers. Throughout my Presidency, organization membership grew by over 150%, we doubled the size of the officer team, increased the number of hosted industry and outreach events, and founded several new programs through SWE including WEHack and the Ladies in Tech Mentoring Program, UTD's first peer to peer mentoring program for women engineers.

WEHack UTD

In November 2019, I Co-Founded Women Engineers Hack (WEHack), UT Dallas' first hackathon for women and non-binary people. The goal of WEHack is to create an inclusive environment that highlights and empowers women students through technology. We expect to have 500 participants at the virtual hackathon in October 2020.

IEEE UTD

During my time as an officer with the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) at UTD between Fall 2017 and Spring 2020, I served as the Public Relations Director and subsequently the Director of Operations for the organization. As Director of Operations, I founded the Events Committee, which put together all corporate and outreach events for IEEE UTD. My committee also handled corporate relations and sponsorship.
